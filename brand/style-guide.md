# The Lost Bureau — Brand Style Guide

**Tagline:** Forgotten Knowledge, Documented  
**Visual Aesthetic:** Retro-government (1960s-70s bureaucratic) + Mysterious Archive  
**Voice:** Authoritative, Curious, Slightly Conspiratorial  

---

## Color Palette

See `colors.json` for exact hex values and usage.

### Primary Colors
- **Dark Charcoal (#2D2D2D):** Main background, authority
- **Gold (#D4AF37):** Prestige badges, seals, highlights
- **Black (#1A1A1A):** Text, dark accents

### Secondary Colors
- **Deep Red (#8B0000):** Danger/forbidden emphasis
- **Off-White (#F5F1E8):** Text on dark, vintage paper feel
- **Medium Gray (#5C5C5C):** Neutral balance, dividers

## Typography

### Headings (Official Authority)
- **Font:** Georgia or Garamond (serif)
- **Size:** 48pt+ for main titles
- **Weight:** Bold or Regular
- **Color:** Gold (#D4AF37) or Off-White (#F5F1E8)
- **Letter-spacing:** +2px (official, formal look)
- **Style:** All-caps for section headers (when appropriate)

**Example:**
```
THE LOST BUREAU
FORGOTTEN KNOWLEDGE, DOCUMENTED
```

### Body Text (Modern Clarity)
- **Font:** Inter or Roboto (sans-serif)
- **Size:** 14-18pt for descriptions, 12pt for small text
- **Weight:** Regular for body, Semi-bold for emphasis
- **Color:** Off-White (#F5F1E8) on dark backgrounds
- **Line-height:** 1.6 for readability
- **Contrast:** Maintain 7:1 contrast ratio for accessibility

### Accent Text (Emphasis)
- **Font:** Same as body (Inter/Roboto)
- **Weight:** Bold (700)
- **Color:** Gold (#D4AF37) for highlights, Deep Red (#8B0000) for warnings

### Layout
- **Left (25%):** Vintage filing cabinets + archive imagery (20% opacity)

- **Right (25%):** Classified stamps, archive documents (20% opacity)
- **Bottom Accent:** Full-width gold bar (60px height, #D4AF37)

### Text Overlay (Optional)
- "The Lost Bureau" in Garamond 80pt, gold, center-right

### Design Notes
- Add subtle film grain texture across entire banner
- Use vignette effect (darker edges) to focus center
- Maintain dark charcoal background (#2D2D2D)

## Thumbnail Style (1280x720px)

### Formula
1. **Background:** Dark charcoal (#2D2D2D) or black (#1A1A1A)
2. **Main Image:** 50-60% of thumbnail (35% opacity, archive/vintage photo)
3. **Bold Text:** Video title in white or gold, 40pt minimum
4. **Accent Shapes:** Gold/red bars or borders (frame the text)
5. **Logo:** "THE LOST BUREAU" small version (bottom-left corner, 50x50px)
6. **Visual Hierarchy:** Text > Image > Logo

### Text Guidelines
- **Font:** Bold sans-serif (Impact, Arial Black, or Roboto Bold)
- **Color:** White with gold shadow, or gold with black outline
- **Line limit:** 2-3 lines maximum
- **Placement:** Centered or upper-right (avoid logo corner)

### Image Selection
- Historical, mysterious, or official-looking imagery
- Archive photographs, government buildings, vintage documents
- Low saturation (desaturate to 50-80%)
- Slight color cast: warm (sepia) or cool (blueprint blue)

### Emoji/Icons
- Optional small emoji top-right (⚠️, 🔒, 📂, ⏰)
- Use sparingly, only if it enhances clarity

### Examples of Good Thumbnails
✅ Dark background + Gold text "ILLEGAL SKILL" + Old archive photo  
✅ Black background + Red warning text + Classified stamp + Bureau logo  
✅ Charcoal + White title + Vintage government building + Gold border  

### Examples of Bad Thumbnails
❌ Too much text (hard to read on mobile)  
❌ Bright colors (clashes with dark aesthetic)  
❌ Tiny logo or text (loses clarity at small size)  
❌ No clear focal point (viewer doesn't know where to look)

## Visual Mood & Tone

### Aesthetic
- **Era:** 1960s-1970s government/bureaucracy aesthetic
- **Texture:** Film grain, vintage paper, typewriter marks, classified stamps
- **Lighting:** Slightly dim, mysterious, archive-like
- **Color Temperature:** Warm sepia or cool blueprint tones

### Imagery Selection
**Preferred:**
- Declassified government documents
- Archive photographs
- Historical maps and blueprints
- Vintage office/bureaucracy scenes
- Scientific or technical equipment
- Official seals and badges

**Avoid:**
- Modern bright colors
- Current day settings
- Comic Sans or casual fonts
- Bright fluorescent lighting

## Motion & Animation (if applicable)

### Channel Trailer / Intro Sequence
- **Duration:** 3-5 seconds
- **Style:** Documentary-like reveals
- **Elements:**
  - Bureau seal fades in from black
  - "THE LOST BUREAU" text appears in gold
  - Classified stamp stamps down
  - Fade to black with voiceover intro
- **Music:** Suspenseful, orchestral (low volume)

### Video Section Headers
- Black screen with gold text (2-3 seconds)
- Simple wipe transition (no busy effects)
- Example: "CHAPTER 2: THE SUPPRESSION"

## Voice & Tone (Non-Visual Branding)

### Overall Voice
- **Authoritative:** You know your subject matter
- **Curious:** Investigative, question-driven
- **Slightly Conspiratorial:** Hint of "what they don't want you knowing"
- **Professional:** Not sensationalist, but dramatic

### Writing Style
- Short declarative sentences mixed with longer explanations
- Ask rhetorical questions to hook audience
- Use "they," "governments," "authorities" (vague, mysterious)
- Avoid caps lock (use *italics* for emphasis instead)
- Conversational, like explaining to a friend

### Example Tones
✅ *"What if everything you knew about this technique was wrong?"*  
✅ *"Governments spent decades trying to erase this from history."*  
✅ *"Here's what they don't want you discovering..."*  

❌ *"THIS IS INSANE!!!! YOU WON'T BELIEVE THIS!!!!!"*  
❌ *"Goverments are literally evil and want to kill you."*

## Consistency Rules

### Across All Platforms
- **Colors:** Always use the gold, charcoal, and off-white combo
- **Fonts:** Garamond for headers, Roboto/Inter for body
- **Logo:** Appear in every thumbnail, banner, and profile
- **Tone:** Authoritative, curious, mysterious

### Social Media Consistency
- Same logo and color scheme on TikTok, Instagram, Twitter snippets
- Same voiceover style and pacing
- Same title formula and keyword strategy

### Channel Evolution
- This style guide is version 1.0
- Update style guide if major rebranding occurs
- Always maintain the "retro government" aesthetic as core identity
